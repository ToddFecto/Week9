Developing:

My developing background has been limited to going to school. I attended The Computer Learning Center, in San Francisco , back in 1993-1994 mainly focusing on C language in a Unix environment, as well as Cobol and CICS in a Mainframe environment, and Novell. Most recently I attended the University of Phoenix Online where I studied Java, C++, JavaScript, HTML, CSS, XML, as well as database design using MS Access and Business SQL. I finally am fortunate enough to be employed with a company that looks to build on their employees' capabilites and so 10 years after graduating with a computer science degree in Software Engineering I am finally getting to brush the heavy rust off and get to work doing some actual coding - getting that work experience that I lacked upon graduation in 2010.

Hometown:

I grew up in the Green Mountain state of Vermont, in the small southeastern most town of Vernon. It was a town primarily made up of dairy farms and some small businesses. If you were to look it up we were best known as the home of Vermont Yankee - a nuclear power plant that has now been decommissioned. It was a great small town to grow up in with a population less than 2000 residents. I attended Vernon Elementary School from 1st - 6th grade. I grew up helping with gathering hay bails in the summer, before moving on to working at the local recreation park doing cleaning, building and pool maintenance and upkeep. I moved from Vernon when I was 20 years old, after graduating from Vermont Technical College with a degree in Electrical/Electronics Engineering Technology and then joining the United States Army, as a Satellite Communications Operator/Maintainer, in 1986. I served 7 years and was stationed in High Wycombe, England for 4 years and finished at the Sacramento Army Depot, in 1993.
